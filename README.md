# Ambient Scribe — Indian Players

A curated landscape scan of ambient clinical documentation tools, AI medical scribes, and voice-to-discrete-data (V2DD) products operating in or relevant to the Indian healthcare market.

---

## What this repository contains

| File | Description |
|------|-------------|
| `V2DD_Excel_Compendium.xlsx` | Master compendium of 16 mapped players with per-product evidence on pricing, Indian language support, ambient vs. dictation classification, multi-speaker capability, output/discrete-data depth, validation evidence, and due-diligence questions |

---

## Scope

The scan covers tools positioned for **ambient encounter capture**, **voice-to-prescription**, **medical dictation**, and **voice-to-structured EMR / discrete data** in India. Products span India-built startups (e.g., Augnito, KiraDoc, RxNote, MedoriaX, EkaScribe, Merago), India-facing SaaS (QModify, VivaLyn, CurePilot, VoxDox), and international benchmarks (Suki AI, Fireflies.ai).

**Prepared:** May 2026  
**Total players mapped:** 16

---

## Key findings at a glance

- **True ambient or ambient-style encounter capture** (multi-speaker, free-flowing doctor–patient conversation): 3 products with strong public evidence; several others claim it without confirming multi-speaker diarisation details.
- **Indian language support:** English output is universal. Explicit Hindi/Hinglish support is claimed across all 16. Bengali/Bangla and Tamil/Kannada/Telugu/Malayalam coverage varies considerably — see the `Landscape` sheet for per-product detail.
- **Independent validation gap:** Publicly available, peer-reviewed clinical validation for Indian-language ambient scribing was generally not found. Vendor accuracy claims (e.g., 98–99% transcription accuracy) are recorded separately from independent evidence.
- **Pricing:** Only QModify and Fireflies.ai publish clear public tariffs. Most India-facing medical scribe products are contact-sales or demo-gated.

## How to use this for procurement / research

1. **Start with the `Summary` sheet** for a quick count of players by capability.
2. **Filter the `Landscape` sheet** by `Ambient vs dictation classification` to isolate true ambient products from dictation/voice-to-Rx tools.
3. **Check the language columns** (Hindi, Bengali, Tamil, Kannada, Telugu, Malayalam) — `Yes` = explicit public claim; `Likely` = broad claim without itemised list; `Not public` = no evidence found.
4. **Use the `Key limitations / due diligence questions` column** to structure vendor demos and RFP questions.
5. **Cross-check `Validation evidence found publicly`** — the absence of independent validation is the dominant procurement risk across this landscape.

---

## Caveats

- Public websites and marketing pages were the primary evidence source; this is a desk-based scan, not a live demo or clinical pilot.
- Product capabilities can change rapidly; re-verify key claims before procurement decisions.
- Some players (e.g., PenBOT, ScribeMed) appear at prototype or early-stage SaaS maturity — treat with additional diligence.
- DPDPA (India's Digital Personal Data Protection Act) compliance was explicitly claimed by only one player (Merago); others require direct vendor confirmation.
